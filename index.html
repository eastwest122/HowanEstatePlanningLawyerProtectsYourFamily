    What Does a Probate Lawyer Actually Do?

    A probate lawyer (sometimes called an estate lawyer or probate attorney) helps settle a person’s estate after they pass away. Their main responsibilities include:

  • Validating the will in court

  • Identifying and valuing assets

  • Paying debts and taxes

  • Distributing property to heirs and beneficiaries

  • Resolving disputes among family members or other parties

    Why You Need a Probate Lawyer in New York

    In New York State, probate is handled in the Surrogate’s Court of the county where the deceased lived — for residents of Flushing, that means the Queens County Surrogate’s Court.
